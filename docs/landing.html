What if reality really is infinite?

Not metaphorically.

Not poetically.

Actually infinite.

No edge you eventually bump into.

No floor where things finally stop dividing.

No foundation holding it all up.

If that's true—if there's genuinely no solid ground anywhere—

then how does anything persist at all?

Why isn't everything just... flux?

Forever?

I followed that question for three years.

And I found something strange.

The same answer kept showing up.

In physics. In biology. In ancient Chinese.

In the way good conversations work.

In the structure of a wheel.

The same pattern. Everywhere.

Stable structures don't stand on foundations.

They circulate around centers that stay empty.

Thirty spokes share one hub.

It's the hole in the center that makes it useful.

— Tao Te Ching, Chapter 11

An atom is mostly empty space.

The center can't be pinned down. That's not a problem—it's the design.

The core can rot away completely.

The tree keeps standing. The center was never what held it up.

Three different domains.

Three different centuries.

Same structure.

Hollow center.
Circulation around it.
Stability through orbit, not anchor.

Once I saw it, I couldn't unsee it.

This site is an invitation to look with me.

Not to believe anything.

Just to notice.
