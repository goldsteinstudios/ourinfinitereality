Life at the Boundary
self / other — The pattern made flesh (and wood)

A tree can have a hollow center and still stand. The pith rots away; the tree lives. Why? Because life doesn't happen at the center—it happens at the boundary. The cambium, the layer where all growth occurs, has zero thickness. Chapter 11 written in wood.

The Central Image

Cut a tree open. The heartwood can be entirely hollow—and the tree thrives. Growth rings around emptiness. The void at the center IS the structure. This isn't metaphor. It's biology demonstrating the pattern.
