# Our Infinite Reality

**Structural translations of the Dao De Jing**

---

## What This Is

This is an ongoing attempt to read the Dao De Jing as technical documentation rather than philosophy.

The working hypothesis: the 81 chapters encode observations about how stable patterns persist in an infinitely divisible reality. Not mystical wisdom. Not moral instruction. Engineering notes written in characters whose radicals function as type signatures.

The translations here treat each character as a compound of operations—radicals that encode substrate and transformation—rather than as words to be rendered into equivalent English words.

## The Approach

**Traditional translation:** Find the English word that best matches the Chinese word.

**Structural translation:** Decompose characters into radicals, identify the operations they encode, and document the pattern they describe.

Example:

| Character | Traditional | Structural |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| 利 (lì) | "benefit" or "advantage" | 禾 (grain) + 刂 (blade) = scythe operation; path-cutting through field |
| 治 (zhì) | "govern" or "rule" | 氵 (water) + 台 (platform) = build platforms that support water's self-organization |

The traditional readings aren't wrong—they're downstream. The structural readings attempt to recover what the characters document before the moral/philosophical overlay.

## How to Read This

**If you want the translations:** Start with the [Chapters](/translations/README.md). Each includes Chinese text, pinyin, structural translation, and analysis.

**If you want the framework:** Read the [Framework Synthesis](/translations/meta/framework_synthesis_2025-12-07.md) for the complete model, or [Chapter 25 - Rosetta Stone](/translations/meta/chapter25_rosetta_stone_2025-12-07.md) for the key insight that unlocks the rest.

**If you want the vocabulary:** The [Lexicon](/translations/lexicon/00_lexicon_introduction.md) catalogs characters by operational family.

**If you want archaeological grounding:** The [Guodian manuscripts](/translations/archaeology/guodian_bundle_a_inventory.md) (~300 BCE) provide textual evidence predating later systematization.
